Purpose of Controls

Internal controls safeguard assets, ensure accurate financial reporting, and promote regulatory compliance. Every control is subject to a cost-benefit analysis ‚ÄĒ the resources spent to implement it must be justified by the benefits it provides.

Segregation of Duties ‚ÄĒ ARC

This is a primary risk management strategy to prevent fraud and errors by ensuring no single person controls an entire transaction. Three critical functions that must always be separated: Authorization (A), Recordkeeping (R), and Custody (C) of assets.

The Five Accounting Cycles

Activities are broken into structured cycles: Sales/Receivables, Cash Receipts, Purchases/Payables, Cash Disbursements, and Payroll.

ūüďĆ Key Terms & Definitions

Lockbox System ‚ÄĒ A bank service where customer payments are mailed to a special PO box instead of the organization, expediting funds and improving control.

Blind Copy ‚ÄĒ A purchase order copy sent to Receiving with quantities left blank, forcing employees to physically count items upon arrival.

Tickler File ‚ÄĒ A file organized by due dates acting as a reminder system; used in accounts payable to ensure bills are paid on time to capture discounts.

Imprest Account ‚ÄĒ A bank account that receives deposits exactly equal to planned disbursements; heavily used in payroll processing.

ūüéĮ Core Concepts
Purpose of Management Controls

Management controls guide, monitor, and evaluate organizational activities using tools like performance reviews, budgets, and forecasting. They ensure day-to-day operations align with strategic goals.

Roles and Responsibilities

The CEO establishes the "tone at the top." The board demonstrates its commitment to ethics through its selection of senior management. Everyone in the entity is involved in internal control and is expected to report poor or failing controls to superiors.

Internal Audit's Role

Internal auditors evaluate the internal control system and serve in an advisory role. To maintain strict independence, they must never be responsible for selecting or executing those controls.

Organization and Policies

Organizational structure should clearly define responsibilities, keeping the chain of authority as simple and flexible as possible. A highly developed ethical culture can reduce the need for formal written policies.

ūüďĆ Key Terms & Definitions

Management Controls ‚ÄĒ Tools and processes implemented to track financial progress and operational effectiveness.

Policy ‚ÄĒ Any stated principle that requires, guides, or restricts action within the organization.

Procedures ‚ÄĒ The specific methods of performing activities in conformity with prescribed policies.

Budget ‚ÄĒ A statement of expected results expressed in numerical terms; acts as a control standard for resource input and operational output.

‚ö†ÔłŹ Exam Traps & Frequently Tested
Frequently Tested

Division of internal control responsibilities: management designs the controls ‚Üí everyone executes them ‚Üí internal audit only evaluates them.

Tricky Questions

Reports should emphasize exceptions. A timely report based partly on estimates is often more useful than a delayed report that is perfectly precise.

Common Wrong Assumption

Auditors cannot design a control to fix a weakness they find. Internal auditors cannot select or execute controls without impairing their independence.

ūüí° Exam Tips
‚úÖ Bank reconciliations must always be prepared by a third party to independently detect discrepancies between recorded deposits and bank statements.
‚úÖ Access to physical assets must be limited to personnel responsible for custody ‚ÄĒ strictly away from those responsible for accounting or authorization.
‚úÖ Periodically rotating staff responsibilities (e.g., rotating buyer assignments) is a highly effective control to prevent fraudulent vendor relationships.

The Big Three Frameworks

COSO (governance, risk, compliance) ¬∑ COBIT 2019 (IT governance) ¬∑ ISO 31000 (broad risk principles) ‚ÄĒ the most recognized frameworks used to ensure robust internal controls.

COSO Objectives

Internal control provides reasonable assurance across three categories: Operations, Reporting, and Compliance. Controls are more likely to ensure reporting and compliance objectives (internally manageable) than operational ones (subject to unpredictable external factors).

The Five COSO Components ‚ÄĒ CRIME

Five integrated components that must work together: Control Activities ¬∑ Risk Assessment ¬∑ Information & Communication ¬∑ Monitoring Activities ¬∑ control Environment.

COBIT 2019

An internationally accepted IT governance system built on the reality that IT pervades every facet of operations. Structured around 6 Governance System principles (e.g., providing stakeholder value, tailoring to organizational needs) and 3 Governance Framework principles (e.g., open, flexible, aligned with major standards).

ūüďĆ Key Terms & Definitions

Internal Control ‚ÄĒ A continuous process, affected by the board, management, and personnel, designed to provide reasonable (but not absolute) assurance regarding organizational objectives.

Control Environment ‚ÄĒ The foundational standard establishing the "tone at the top," reflecting management's and the board's commitment to integrity, structure, and ethical values.

Control Activities ‚ÄĒ Specific preventive or detective policies, procedures, and verifications (including segregation of duties) established to mitigate risks.
